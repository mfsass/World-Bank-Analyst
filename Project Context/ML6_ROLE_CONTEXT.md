## What the Incubation Unit Is

The Incubation unit is where ML6 builds new internal capabilities and products. It sits at the intersection of internal R&D and applied engineering — closer to a product studio than a client delivery team.

The AI Native WoW Team — where this role lives — is an internal consultancy tasked with transforming how ML6 itself operates. Their mandate: identify tasks across ML6's business units that can be replaced or augmented by AI, then build the systems to do it.

This is not classic consulting work. This is engineering for operational transformation. The output is not decks or strategies — it is working systems running in production.

---

## The Five Impact Areas

These are what ML6 says the role will actually do:

**1. Process Analysis & Mapping**  
Deep-dives into existing processes across ML6's business units. Identifying bottlenecks and automation opportunities. Requires both analytical rigour and the interpersonal skill to understand how teams actually work.

**2. AI-Native Workflow Design**  
Architecting the "To-Be" state — making strategic decisions about whether to use existing AI tooling or build custom. This is systems thinking, not just tool selection.

**3. System Engineering**  
Building agentic systems and complex workflows using advanced prompt engineering. The technical core of the role.

**4. Solution Delivery**  
End-to-end AI systems covering data engineering, software development, and sophisticated AI components. Full-stack delivery, not partial.

**5. Adoption & Change Management**  
Ensuring new systems are actually used. Not just implemented — fully exploited by teams. This is the "bridge" function — between technical capability and human behaviour.

## The Interview Process

### Stage 1 — Get to Know
Initial call with a recruiter (Julie Plusquin). They provide materials upfront and recommend candidates go through them before the call. This implies they expect candidates to have done research and be prepared to discuss ML6's work, not just their own background.

### Stage 2 — Presentation Time (The Challenge)
This is where the World Analyst challenge lives. 

Format:
- `[05 min]` Round table introductions
- `[30 min]` Presentation of your solution
- `[15 min]` Q&A

The challenge evaluates "skills and creativity" against the "key criteria for the future role." Multiple ML6 people ("agents") are involved — not just the recruiter. This is a technical panel, not a single interviewer.

The Q&A is where Absolute Ownership is tested. Expect every architectural decision to be challenged.

### Stage 3 — Final Interview
Retrospective on the candidate journey. Interview with unit coaches and/or founders. This is the cultural fit and strategic fit conversation — expect questions about how you think about AI's role in business operations, not just technical execution.

## Connection to the Unum Platform

The AI Automation Engineer role is part of the Incubation unit, which is the same unit building and operating Unum.

Understanding Unum is essential context for this role:

- Unum is ML6's enterprise superintelligence platform — an AI-native operating system that generates agents to run business processes
- The WoW Team's work feeds into Unum's development — internal automation wins become product capabilities
- The role is effectively building proof-of-concept systems that validate and extend the Unum vision

This means the World Analyst project is not just a technical exercise. It is a demonstration of the kind of thinking that goes into Unum's internal development: structured reasoning, intent-first design, observable pipeline architecture, and production-grade output from the first commit.

## What Sets This Role Apart from Standard Engineering

This is not a role where writing good code is enough.

ML6 is explicitly building the AI Native WoW — and the person in this role is partly responsible for defining what that looks like inside the company. That requires:

- The credibility to walk into any business unit and understand their workflow within hours
- The engineering depth to design and build a solution that solves the real problem, not just the stated one
- The communication skill to make the solution stick — to drive adoption, not just delivery
- The AI fluency to stay ahead of the tooling landscape and make opinionated choices about what to build vs buy

The World Analyst project is a compressed, 30-minute demonstration of all four.

## What ML6 Finds Impressive (Inferred from Their Own Writing)

From the challenge brief, blog posts, and role description, these are the signals ML6 responds to:

- **Seamless access** — if your solution requires 12 steps to demo, you have already missed the brief
- **The "why" is explicit** — not just what you built, but every decision that led there
- **Production thinking in a prototype context** — scale-to-zero, secrets management, region selection, structured API contracts
- **AI used deliberately** — structured prompting with clear intent, not single-pass generation
- **Cross-functional thinking** — the system serves a stakeholder (ML6 engineers), not just a spec
- **Honest scope** — acknowledging trade-offs (e.g., "Firestore supports BigQuery migration if analytical queries emerge") shows engineering maturity
